Pricing

Credit and Rebates

  • Credit
  • Residential Clean Energy Credit
  • 30% of the costs of clean energy equipment (years 2022-2032)
  • 26% of the costs of clean energy equipment (year 2033)
  • 22% of the costs of clean energy equipment (year 2034)
  • Amount that is received in credit cannot be more than what is owed in taxes
  • Excess credit can be saved for the future (can be used for taxes in following years)
  • Fuel Cell Property
  • $500 per 1/2 kilowatt fuel cell power
  • If a home is inhabited by more than one person, the total credit cannot surpass $1667 per 1/2 kilowett of fuel cell power
  • Rebates
  • Rebates are deducted from costs if...
  • Rebate is determined by the cost of the property
  • Rebate is connected to a person associated with the sale (ex. manufacturer)
  • Rebate isn't given for work you've done

Credit Eligibility

  • Owner Eligibility
  • Home must be the main home (where you live for most of the time)
  • Rented or owned
  • If it's a second home, it must follow certain parameters
  • Homes sometimes used for business details
  • Home used 20% of the time for business --> all credit available
  • Home used over 20% of the time for business --> credits based on nonbusiness costs
  • Property Elligibility
  • Solar electric panels
  • Solar water heaters
  • Required certification from Solar Rating Certification Corporation or other organization approved by your state
  • Wind turbines
  • Geothermal heat pumps
  • Required Energy Star specifications that were necessary at the time it was purchased
  • Fuel cells
  • Battery storage tech (from 2023 or later)
  • Required power of 3 kilowatt hours or more
  • NOT QUALIFYING: previously owned technology
  • Credit can possibly be gained from installation costs
